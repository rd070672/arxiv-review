Paper: 2603.25659
Title: "Diffusion in interacting two-dimensional systems under a uniform magnetic field"

Authors: Łukasz Iwanek, Marcin Mierzejewski (First authors identified)

Submitted: 26 Mar 2026 (Today's date: 28 Mar 2026, ~2 days old)

Category: cond-mat.quant-gas

Primary Broad Area: 非平衡ダイナミクス (Non-equilibrium Dynamics)
Secondary Broad Area: 計算材料科学 (Computational Materials Science)

Abstract Summary:
The paper investigates a fundamentally challenging problem: how do interacting particles
diffuse in two-dimensional lattices when subjected to orbital magnetic fields? Using the
fermionic truncated Wigner approximation (fTWA), the authors study fermionic diffusion
at infinite temperature in systems with competing effects:
- Hopping energy (kinetic)
- Interaction strength (potential)
- Magnetic field strength (geometric)

Key Results:
1. When interaction strength >> hopping energy: magnetic field effects are suppressed
2. When interaction ≈ hopping energy: magnetic flux dramatically reduces diffusion
3. Effects become clear in large systems (L > 20 sites) where finite-size limitations disappear
4. fTWA provides unexpectedly good agreement with exact numerics for intermediate interactions
5. Predictions are experimentally testable using ultracold fermi gases in optical lattices

Methodology Highlights:
- Fermionic Truncated Wigner Approximation (fTWA): Main computational tool
  * Allows efficient simulation of large, interacting systems
  * Captures nonequilibrium dynamics without explicit diagonalization

- Lanczos Exact Diagonalization: Benchmarking on small systems
  * Validates fTWA accuracy across parameter regimes

- Optical Lattice Feasibility: Discussed as experimental platform
  * Suggests direct testability in cold atom experiments

Why This Topic is Important:
- Fundamental challenge: transport in strongly-correlated 2D systems
- Practical relevance: quantum simulators, materials with strong interactions
- Methodological advance: validates fTWA for new physical regimes
- Experimental pathway: identified clear experimental realization scheme

Article Structure Recommendation:
1. Why magnetic transport in 2D is hard (intro)
2. Competition between interactions and magnetic fields (problem statement)
3. What does fTWA predict? (main results)
4. How does this connect to related theories? (context with 7 related papers)
5. Can we test this? (experimental prospects)
