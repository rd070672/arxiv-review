Title: Diffusion in interacting two-dimensional systems under a uniform magnetic field
Authors: Łukasz Iwanek, Marcin Mierzejewski (et al.)
Submitted: 26 Mar 2026

Abstract:
The dynamics of interacting particles in orbital magnetic fields are notoriously difficult to study,
as this physics is inherently connected to electronic correlations in two-dimensional systems, for
which no straightforward theoretical methods are available. The authors report on the diffusion of
interacting fermionic particles in 2D lattices under uniform magnetic fields at infinite temperature
using the fermionic truncated Wigner approximation (fTWA).

Main Methodology:
- Fermionic truncated Wigner approximation (fTWA)
- Lanczos numerical diagonalization (for benchmarking)
- Optical lattice platforms (for experimental feasibility)

Key Findings:
1. Strong interactions exceeding hopping energy suppress magnetic field effects on transport
2. When interactions match kinetic energy scales, magnetic flux significantly reduces diffusion
3. Effects become prominent in larger systems (400+ lattice sites) where finite-size effects diminish
4. Results should be experimentally feasible using optical lattice platforms
5. fTWA captures equilibration dynamics well for intermediate interaction strengths

Research Significance:
This work addresses a fundamental challenge in understanding quantum many-body dynamics under
magnetic fields—a problem relevant to both condensed matter physics and quantum simulation
experiments with ultracold atoms. The validated applicability of fTWA at intermediate interaction
strengths provides a valuable computational tool for studying non-equilibrium phenomena in
2D interacting systems.
